Search for Exoplants in Compact Binary Systems

System NN Serpentis

In recent decades, some systems composed of two stars (binary system) showed cyclical variation of their orbital periods. This variation is generally explained by the light-time effect, which is a periodic effect caused by the gravitational interaction between the binary system and circumbinary bodies. In this work, we use photometric data obtained in 2019 at the Pico Observatory of the days of the NN Serpentis system, which is an eclipsing binary system composed of a white dwarf and a red dwarf. Thus, after reducing the photometric data and analyzing the light curve of this binary system, we obtained the measurement of the minimum instant of the eclipse. Our result diverges from the solution proposed in the literature of two circumbinary planets in NN Serpentis. We conclude that more data will be needed to arrive at a univocal solution about the variation of the orbital period of this system.
